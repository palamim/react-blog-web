I'm gonna make a case for why traditional programming is more fun than vibe coding with AI. At least for me.

We, old-school devs, cannot run away. <mark>Vibe coding</mark> is the vibe in 2026. AI IDEs, Claude Code, billion-dollar apps in under 5 hours.

```
> hey claude, build me a billion dollar app, be concise and precise, don't use many tokens
```

AI completely changed how we build things. But it's also changing how we _think_ while we build. And I'm not sure that's always good.

I built this blog in Astro using Claude Code. From idea to buying a domain and going live it took me less than 4 hours. Genuinely impressive. But something felt off cause I hadn't really _built_ anything. Claude had.

So I went out and rebuilt this same app from scratch in raw React with no AI and no autocomplete. Just docs, Stack Overflow, and my hands.

_ps: you're reading the Astro app right now. The React rebuild is over on my [Github](https://github.com/palamim/react-blog-web) if you're curious._

That's when I remembered what was actually good about programming. <mark>Knowing</mark> why you did something. Being <mark>curious</mark> about how TypeScript works. Being <mark>diligent</mark> about typing things the way you know you have to.

When was the last time you wrote a `useState` hook without an AI completing it? Or defined a TS interface from scratch?

Building this the "old way" has been the most <mark>vibe</mark> I've had coding in months. Knowing why JSX was created in the first place can make a man go to bed and sleep like a happy, fulfilled baby.

> Don't make me build this in raw JavaScript. I might do it.

More notes as I keep raw coding.
